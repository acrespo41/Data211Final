COVID-19 Impacts on Stocks

Effects of the COVID-19 Pandemic on Industry Stock Prices

Overview and Purpose

Our project analyzes the daily adjusted close stock prices of six industries during the period of January 2019 to April 2021 to visualize the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. An adjusted close stock price is the final price for a stock traded during market hours revised to reflect the stock’s true value and accounts for corporate actions. Our purpose is to understand and assess how the pandemic affected industries as well as analyze the variation within these industries. This investigation can be a tool to study monetary impacts on different parts of the economy and to support efficient distribution of government relief funds.

We chose to observe the technology, pharmaceutical, airline, hotel, restaurant, and alcohol industries with the expectation that they are likely to be greatly affected by the pandemic, whether that be positively or negatively. Our comprehensive timeline ensures that we have an accurate examination of stock prices before and after the pandemic’s inception. For all three figures, we averaged the daily adjusted close stock prices of five companies in each industry. This method allows us to get a general understanding of how the industry overall is doing, but it may limit and skew our findings.

Conclusion

With our visualizations, we have achieved our purpose and gained insight into how various industries’ stock prices have changed during the COVID-19 pandemic. However, correlation is not the same as causation. As a result, further analysis is necessary to determine if and to what degree the pandemic has been the cause of these industry changes. If we were to continue with this project in the future, we would include more companies in the data to get a more accurate view of each industry. We would also look into the food industry to compare changes in fast food restaurants with sit-down restaurants. Additionally, we would have liked to look at stay-at-home orders and remote work environments and observe how that may have impacted various industries, especially the tech industry. Overall, our project was successful in reaching our goals and may be helpful for government allocation of pandemic relief aid.
